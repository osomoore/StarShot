StarBreach Rules

A cooperative expansion for StarShot

Overview

In StarBreach, one to four players work together against a massive alien StarBreacher and its supporting fleet. Instead of competing with each other, the players must accomplish the scenario objective before the StarBreacher completes its own.

Unless otherwise stated below, all normal StarShot rules remain in effect.

Scenario: Bauble Breacher
Player Objective

One player is designated The Prey at the start of the game.

The players win if:

The Prey ends Round 6 inside The Fang.
Boss Objective

The StarBreacher attempts to prevent this by eliminating The Prey.

If the Prey is destroyed, the players immediately lose.

Player Roles

Each player chooses one role.

If playing with fewer than four players, one player may control multiple roles.

Bauble Runner
Move distances are doubled on basic movement.
This doubling is not increased in Overdrive.
Movement gives no defense bonus.
Whenever this player collects a Bauble, every player draws one bonus card.
Tank
Begins with one additional Shield Charge.
Proximity Jammer: While within three hexes of an enemy, that enemy targets the Tank instead of any other valid target. Enemy attacks against the Tank roll one fewer attack die.
Engineer
Draw two additional cards during Draw.
Attack Orders may target allies instead of enemies.
Repairs are resolved as attacks using 1d6 instead of 2d6.
A successful repair restores one HP instead of dealing damage.
A ship may be repaired at most once per action.
Fighting Ace

Choose one of the following each time an attack is made:

Roll one additional attack die, or
Modify the Boss Damage Lane roll by ±1.

Additionally:

Ignore Overdrive penalties on Attack-only Orders.

Facing does not matter.

Each craft simply tracks remaining HP until destroyed.

The StarBreacher

The StarBreacher is represented by a large hex-grid ship.

See the supplied ship diagram starbreach_boss_scenario_01.jpg in the docs/rules.

The ship is not divided into independent health zones.

Instead, every hex is part of one continuous structure.

The labeled areas simply determine:

which Damage Lane table is used,
which Shield Generators protect that area,
and which components belong to that section.

The Bauble Breacher ship contains four functional regions:

Forward Shields
Port Cannons
Rear Engines
Starboard Cannons
Ship Components

Certain hexes represent important systems.

See the supplied ship diagram for component locations.

Shield Generators

Three Shield Generators protect portions of the ship.

Each generator provides a 3 HP shield to its assigned shield arc.

Destroying a generator immediately removes that shield.

Cannons

Four Cannons control the StarBreacher's Attack action slots.

Each cannon is linked to one specific Boss Action Slot.

When destroyed, that action slot is permanently disabled.

Engines

Four Engines control the StarBreacher's Move action slots.

Each engine is linked to one specific Boss Action Slot.

When destroyed, that action slot is permanently disabled.

Breacher Core

The Core appears on the ship diagram for future scenarios.

It has no gameplay function in this implementation.

Attacking the StarBreacher

Players may attack either:

enemy fleet craft, or
the StarBreacher.

Fleet attacks resolve exactly like normal StarShot attacks.

Boss attacks are resolved differently.

Choose a target ship area.
Resolve the attack normally.
On a successful hit, roll the area's Damage Lane die.
The indicated damage lane determines which hull hex is struck (see the supplied ship diagram).
Damage is applied directly to that hex.

If a lane reaches a destroyed hull section, continue inward along that lane until the first intact hex is found.

Damage Lanes

Each ship area has seven damage lanes.

Roll a d8.

2–7: Strike the corresponding damage lane.
1: Glancing Blow.

A Glancing Blow does not strike the ship.

Instead, the attacking player gains one Desperation card.

The Fighting Ace may modify the lane roll by ±1 after rolling.

Boss Progress Track

The StarBreacher advances along its Progress Track during play.

In this scenario:

Each successful hit against The Prey advances the track by 1.
Destroying The Prey advances the track by 3.

Certain spaces grant new capabilities.

Standard Tier spaces unlock additional Boss Action Slots.

Boss Tier spaces will be used by future Boss abilities.

See the scenario board for the exact progression track.

it is a Base slot,

The StarBreach phase has no Base action.

As components are destroyed, linked action slots are removed.

As Progress Tiers are reached, Tier slots become active.

The boss's available actions therefore increase through progression and decrease as players destroy critical systems.

Resolving Boss Actions

Each active slot performs one action.

The action type depends on its phase.

Action 0.5 — Attack
Action 1.5 — Move
Action 2.5 — Move
Action 3.5 — Attack

For every active slot:

Roll a d3 (0,0,1,1,2,2).
Add 1.

Attack phases perform that many attacks.

Move phases move that many hexes.

Each slot is resolved independently.
